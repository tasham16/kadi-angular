Abstract
Of the many dimensions of the universal issue of resource distribution, one – the allocation of healthcare resources, particularly those for mental healthcare – stands out.
In Kenya, mental healthcare falls low on the government’s list of priorities meaning that mental healthcare services are especially poor. This is a cause for concern, especially due to the fact that neighborhood deprivation is linked with the prevalence of mental illness.
The Goal for this research was to create a deprivation index that measures socio-economic disadvantage in different counties in Kenya and compile a dataset consisting of mental health resources within each region in order to assess the distribution.
